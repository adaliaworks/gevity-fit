How it works

Built for the long game.
Not the highlight reel.

Most fitness apps are designed for people who are already motivated. Gevity is designed for the rest of us — we know consistency matters but we need a little help staying on track when life gets in the way.

Day one

Your first 90 days,
already written.

Most apps start empty. An empty app is a quiet accusation — you haven't done anything yet. Gevity starts full.

After a two-minute setup, we pre-load 90 days of your personalised workout history. Your splits, your frequency, your rest days, even a fictional vacation week baked right in. Because that's what the next 90 days actually look like for a normal person.

The conceit is simple: here's what your progress looks like if you just keep going. Now go make it real.

Every workout you log replaces a fictional one. Your history becomes real, one session at a time.

When life happens

Missed a week?
Gevity adjusts with you.

Taking time off — vacation, illness, just needing a break — is part of training. But coming back too hard after a week away is how people get hurt or burned out.

When you return after a gap, Gevity gently suggests dialing back your weights and reps for a session or two. Not as a punishment — as a realistic ramp back up. Your body isn't quite where it was, and your plan should reflect that.

Think of it as the app having a good memory and no judgment. It knows what you were doing, knows you stepped away, and meets you where you actually are.

💡
How we calculate it: We apply a small, time-based reduction to your targets for each missed session — enough to feel different, not enough to feel discouraging. You can always override it if you're feeling strong.

After every workout

The stat that makes
it stick.

When you finish a workout, Gevity shows you something you won't find in any other fitness app: a single number that makes the whole thing feel real.

We add up every pound you moved, multiply by how far it traveled, and give you a comparison that actually means something.

The comparison rotates based on how much you moved — sometimes it's a golden retriever, sometimes it's a bag of concrete, sometimes it's something funnier. The point is the same: you did something real today.
